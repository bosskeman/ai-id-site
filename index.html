One-line promise

“Power without identity creates uncertainty. AI-ID makes AI action attributable, directional, and auditable.”

The problem

Modern AI systems are evolving into agent swarms — millions of autonomous processes interacting. But critical questions remain unanswered:

  • Who does an AI agent act for?
  • Under which identity and authority?
  • With what ethical direction?
  • Where is the responsibility trace when things go wrong?

Why it matters

Without identity, AI action becomes unattributable, governance becomes weak, and trust collapses. The result is systemic uncertainty — not only online, but in real-world decisions.

Unidentified Agents → Untraceable Actions → Ungovernable Systems

The solution: AI-ID Layer

AI-ID introduces a protocol layer that binds every meaningful AI action to: verified identity, declared intent, and ethical direction — producing an auditable responsibility chain.

Identity

Every AI agent must be attributable to a source identity (human, org, or verified system).

Direction

Actions carry declared intent + ethical constraints, not just outputs.

Trace

Every action leaves a responsibility trace to enable audit, governance, and trust.

Architecture (high-level)

AI-ID is designed to be compatible with different stacks (agents, platforms, APIs, governance layers).

1) Identity Anchor - Verified source identity (human/org/system) - Agent permissions & scopes 2) Ethical Direction Layer - Declared intent + constraints - Policy hooks / “Ethics Filter Logic” 3) Responsibility Trace - Signed action records - Auditability & dispute resolution Output: Action → Identity → Direction → Trace

Principles

  • Identity without coercion (digital effect requires ID, biology doesn’t)
  • Direction is explicit (intent + ethics)
  • Trace is default (audit-ready by design)
  • Trust is portable across domains (context-aware)
